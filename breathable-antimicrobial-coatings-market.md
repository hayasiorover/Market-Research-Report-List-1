<p><p>Breathable antimicrobial coatings are designed to inhibit microbial growth while allowing moisture vapor to escape. The market is divided into two primary types: organic and inorganic coatings. Organic coatings typically utilize biocidal agents derived from natural sources, offering a more eco-friendly approach. Inorganic coatings, on the other hand, often incorporate metal oxides or nanoparticles that provide long-lasting antimicrobial properties. Both types serve various applications, including healthcare, textiles, and construction, enhancing hygiene and preventing contamination.</p></p>
