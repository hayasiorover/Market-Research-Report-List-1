<p><p>Geographic Information Systems (GIS) encompass various data types essential for spatial analysis. Numeric data includes quantitative metrics linked to geographic locations, facilitating statistical analysis. Vector data represents geographic features using points, lines, and polygons, ideal for analyzing discrete elements like roads or boundaries. Raster data consists of grid cells or pixels, often used for continuous data like elevation or temperature. Other market types may include 3D data or real-time data layers, enhancing GIS capabilities across various sectors such as urban planning, environmental monitoring, and logistics.</p></p>
