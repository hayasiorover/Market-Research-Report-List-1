<p><p>The foodborne pathogen test kits market comprises various types, primarily focusing on PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) and ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay). PCR is a molecular technique that detects specific DNA sequences from pathogens, allowing for rapid and highly sensitive identification. In contrast, ELISA utilizes antibodies to detect and quantify pathogens, offering a broader range for different microorganisms. Both methods serve crucial roles in ensuring food safety, helping manufacturers and regulators quickly identify contamination and maintain public health standards.</p></p>
