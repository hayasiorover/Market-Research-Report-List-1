<p><p>The bio-based industrial wax market includes two primary types: vegetable waxes and animal waxes. Vegetable waxes, derived from plant sources like palm, soy, and carnauba, are renewable and commonly used in cosmetics, food packaging, and candles. Animal waxes, sourced from bees (beeswax) and other animal byproducts, offer unique properties such as water resistance and adhesive qualities, making them suitable for applications in pharmaceuticals and personal care. Both types emphasize sustainability and environmentally friendly alternatives in industrial applications.</p></p>
